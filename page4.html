Reasons we finally had to sell our cabin:

-Native land reevaulation

The Native Americans needed to check the land for artifacts, this would be at a cost to us.

-Tacoma Power new waterfront requirements

Tacoma Power decided that we would need to have our existing dock checked by an engineer and rebuilt.

-Forest Service assest new taxes to leased land

The Forest Service raised the taxes on leased land in the area.

-More people in the area

After the Cushman resort closed down, people started coming to the bridge by our property. They treated it like an amusement park. There were lots of trespasing and controntatons.

-My father's health declined

My father developed Parkinsons' Disease, and they stopped coming up to Cushman.

-No longer able to keep up with the maintence

After a while, it seemed like the only reason we were coming to the cabin was to do upkeep. It was no longer relaxing.

Final Reflection

In the end this magical place became all about cost, overcrowding and no time. It was with heavy hearts that we decided we needed to sell the cabin.

That was one of my saddest days; the last time we drove away from Lake Cushman. My daughter.
